What are red flags?

Red flags are warning signs of unhealthy, manipulative, or abusive behaviors in relationships. They’re often subtle at first, but noticing them early can help keep you safe.

Classic Red Flags

  • Jealousy disguised as concern: “I just love you so much, I can’t stand to see you talk to anyone else.”
  • Quick involvement / Love bombing: Intense affection or gifts early on, pressuring commitment.
  • Isolating you from friends/family: “You don’t need them, you have me.”
  • Controlling behavior: Dictating what you wear, who you see, or how you spend your money/time.
  • Constant criticism or put-downs: Belittling your looks, intelligence, or interests—“just joking” or not.
  • Blaming you for their actions: “If you hadn’t made me mad, I wouldn’t have yelled.”
  • Explosive temper or mood swings: You feel like you’re walking on eggshells.
  • History of bad relationships: “My exes were all crazy.”
  • Disregard for your boundaries: Pressuring you for sex, commitment, or to “just let it go.”
  • Gaslighting: Denying things you know happened, making you doubt your own memory or sanity.
  • Minimizing or excusing hurtful behavior: “You’re too sensitive.”
  • Threatening self-harm if you leave: Weaponizing their own well-being to keep you stuck.
  • Financial control: Taking your money, restricting access to finances, or making you justify every purchase.

Lesser-Known Red Flags

  • Too much, too soon: Planning your life together on date three, constant texting/check-ins.
  • Backhanded compliments: “You’d be so pretty if you lost weight.”
  • Testing your loyalty: Creating drama or jealousy traps to “see how you react.”
  • Refusing responsibility: “It’s just how I am, because of my past.”
  • Disrespecting your NO: Not taking “no” for an answer, pushing boundaries “as a joke.”
  • Keeping secrets: Sudden phone privacy, vague about past or daily life.
  • Your gut says something is off: Even if you can’t explain why—trust it!

Why Do People Ignore Red Flags?

  • Fear of being alone
  • Belief that love means “fixing” someone
  • Minimizing their own needs
  • Confusing drama with passion
  • Past trauma or normalizing bad behavior
  • Hope that things will change
